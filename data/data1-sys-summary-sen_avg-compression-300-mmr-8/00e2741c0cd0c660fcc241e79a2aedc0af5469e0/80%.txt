Britain's Prince William, the Duke of Cambridge, is going back to work, taking on a new role as an air ambulance pilot The duke will start training in September, a year after leaving the Royal Air Force, where he flew search and rescue helicopters Most air ambulance pilots have a military background like the Duke, who joined a squadron based at RAF Valley after qualifying as a search and rescue pilot in 2010  He undertook 156 search and rescue operations, resulting in 149 people being rescued  He will have his first air ambulance shift in spring 2015 The palace said the pilot role would be the Duke's primary occupation  The East Anglian Air Ambulance is operated by a private company, Bond Air Services, but the Duke will donate his salary to charity
