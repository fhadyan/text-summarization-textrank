 Federal civil rights investigators have found reasonable cause to believe that police in Portland, Oregon, use unnecessary or unreasonable force with persons who have mental illness, the U  Justice Department said The department's civil rights division and U  Attorney's Office in Oregon issued a letter to Portland Mayor Sam Adams stating that local and federal authorities will continue our collaborative relationship to craft sustainable remedies In the 42-page letter, federal officials outline remedies that include training and new policies to investigate alleged police misconduct Investigators found cause to believe that the Portland Police Bureau engages in a pattern or practice of using excessive force in encounters involving people with actual or perceived mental illness  Perez and U  Attorney Amanda Marshall  We also found that PPB employs practices that escalate the use of force where there were clear earlier junctures when the force could have been avoided or minimized
