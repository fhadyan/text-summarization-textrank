In 2012, photographer Matika Wilbur sold everything in her Seattle apartment and hit the road on a cross-country journey Her goal? To photograph individuals from each of the 562 federally recognized Native American tribes in the United States and memorialize their stories Since then, the number of federally recognized tribes has risen to 566, and Wilbur is still crisscrossing the country, immersing herself in the worlds of Native Americans from Brooklyn to the far reaches of Alaska for her ongoing photo series, Project 562 Fans of the series say its timing could not be better
