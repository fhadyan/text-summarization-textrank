Tripoli Libya's interim parliament Sunday approved a new government, led by Prime Minister Ahmed Mitig, in a controversial vote that threatens to deepen the country's political and security crisis A total of 83 of 93 members present voted in favor of Mitig's government Lawmakers in the Islamist-dominated General National Congress (GNC) defied a threat issued by militias allied with renegade general Khalifa Haftar, who promised to storm, raid and arrest members Shortly before the vote in parliament, the EU's special envoy to Libya described the situation as the country's worst crisis since 2011
