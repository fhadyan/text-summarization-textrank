 The 54 men and 14 boys rescued after being found chained this week at an Islamic religious school in Pakistan have been reunited with their families or placed in shelters, authorities said All 14 boys were returned to their families, senior police official Ahsanullah Marwat told CNN Three people who worked at the facility were arrested, but the four men who ran the place were still at large, Marwat said Officials said the facility was part madrassa and part drug-rehab facility, and the captives were chained at night apparently to prevent their escape
