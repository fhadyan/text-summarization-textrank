ISTANBUL, Turkey It's a common sight in the traffic-clogged streets of Istanbul, a city that straddles two continents. Workers next to the spot where subway trains will one day emerge from the tunnel. Opening of the tunnel has been delayed by at least four years by the discovery. A taxi driver, enraged by perpetually gridlocked traffic, stepping out of his car and yelling "Maniac at the man driving the public bus behind him. For decades Istanbul has been growing at a breakneck speed; its population exceeding by some estimates 15 million people. Too bad traffic often moves at a snail's pace. Most residents are quick to tell visitors the city's transport system is overwhelmed. "Istanbul is a dynamically changing city, every year increasing in population says Zeynep Buket, an engineer working with Turkey's transportation ministry.
