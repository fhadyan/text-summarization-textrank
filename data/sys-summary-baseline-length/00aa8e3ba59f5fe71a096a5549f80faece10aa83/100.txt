MOSCOW, Russia Russian space officials say the crew of the Soyuz space ship is resting after a rough ride back to Earth. A South Korean bioengineer was one of three people on board the Soyuz capsule. The craft carrying South Korea's first astronaut landed in northern Kazakhstan on Saturday, 260 miles (418 kilometers) off its mark, they said. Search helicopters took 25 minutes to find the capsule and determine that the crew was unharmed. Interfax reported that the spacecraft's landing was rough.
