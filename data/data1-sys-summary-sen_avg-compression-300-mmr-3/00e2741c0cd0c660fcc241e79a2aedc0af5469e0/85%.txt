Britain's Prince William, the Duke of Cambridge, is going back to work, taking on a new role as an air ambulance pilot The duke will start training in September, a year after leaving the Royal Air Force, where he flew search and rescue helicopters  Their usual landing spots include school playing fields; residential gardens; car parks; beaches and any open space deemed possible by the pilot The palace said the pilot role would be the Duke's primary occupation It's the first time a royal who's in direct line to the throne has taken a civilian job
