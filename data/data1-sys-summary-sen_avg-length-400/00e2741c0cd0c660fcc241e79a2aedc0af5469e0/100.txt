Britain's Prince William, the Duke of Cambridge, is going back to work, taking on a new role as an air ambulance pilot The duke will start training in September, a year after leaving the Royal Air Force, where he flew search and rescue helicopters Most air ambulance pilots have a military background like the Duke, who joined a squadron based at RAF Valley after qualifying as a search and rescue pilot in 2010  He is highly skilled The palace said the pilot role would be the Duke's primary occupation
