The synthetic marijuana that Emily Bauer consumed was likely one of the second generation of synthetic marijuana chemicals.
 These new synthetic marijuana products are not an example of effective legal regulation, but an example of underregulation.
 Oddly enough, the rush to criminalize synthetic marijuana and other synthetic drugs comes at a time when public opinion is dramatically shifting in favor of decriminalizing, and even legally regulating, marijuana.
  After decades of marijuana prohibition, elected officials and the public are finally realizing that regulating the production and sale of marijuana is the best way to reduce the harms of the illicit marijuana market and the harms of marijuana use itself.
 Indeed, drugs, whether marijuana or synthetic marijuana, should not be legally regulated because they are safe they should be legally regulated precisely because they can be harmful.
 Marijuana itself is the most widely consumed illegal drug more than 100 million Americans have used it and 20 million have been arrested for it since 1965..
  People who have tried synthetic marijuana often report psychoactive effects that are comparable to marijuana, but notably less pleasurable.
