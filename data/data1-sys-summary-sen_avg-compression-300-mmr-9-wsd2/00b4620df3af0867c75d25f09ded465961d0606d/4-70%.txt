Federal civil rights investigators have found reasonable cause to believe that police in Portland, Oregon, use unnecessary or unreasonable force with persons who have mental illness, the U.S. Justice Department said.The city will lead efforts to boost community mental health treatment options, such as establishing a 24-hour secure drop-off and walk-in center, that will provide police officers more options when assisting persons experiencing a mental health crisis.In the 42-page letter, federal officials sketch cure that include training and new policies to investigate alleged police wrongdoing.Investigators found cause to believe that the Portland Police Bureau engages in a pattern or practice of using excessive force in encounters involving people with actual or perceived mental illness.The city will revise its use-of-force policies particularly those regarding the use of stun guns to ensure that officers have necessary direction when encountering someone with mental illness or perceive to have mental illness.
