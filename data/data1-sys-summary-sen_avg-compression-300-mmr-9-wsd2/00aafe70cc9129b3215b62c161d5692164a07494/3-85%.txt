Red Bull's four-time world champion has signed a three-year contract with the Scuderia, the oldest team in F1, from 2015..The double move by two of the sport's high profile world champions is the most significant in the driver market this season.Mercedes, Red Bull, Ferrari, Williams, Lotus and Sauber have all confirmed their driver pairings for 2015. There are still seats on offer at McLaren, Force India and Toro Rosso.There's also only one seat up for grabs at Toro Rosso, who will blood 17-year-old Max Verstappen as the youngest F1 driver in history next season.Junior Red Bull driver Carlos Sainz Jr is in pole position for the second seat and the Spaniard has been named as a test driver for Red Bull at the end of season test in Abu Dhabi although Toro Rosso may still retain French racer Jean-Eric Vergne.There are still seats to be filled at McLaren, Force India and Toro Rosso.
