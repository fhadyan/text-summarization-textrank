Paris Investigators trying to determine why an Air France plane crashed mysteriously two years ago have recovered the complete contents of the flight data recorder and the last two hours of cockpit conversation, they announced MondayIt will take several weeks to analyze the data, French air accident experts saidThose two bodies are being examined to see if there is enough DNA to identify them, investigator said, adding that they hope to have results by WednesdayInvestigators also brought an engine and an avionics bay containing computers to the surface, they saidThe pilots of Air France 447 lost contact with air traffic controllers on June 1, 2009, while flying across an area of the Atlantic known for severe turbulence, officials saidThe fuselage was discovered in April with bodies still inside, investigator said The cause of the crash is still not known.
