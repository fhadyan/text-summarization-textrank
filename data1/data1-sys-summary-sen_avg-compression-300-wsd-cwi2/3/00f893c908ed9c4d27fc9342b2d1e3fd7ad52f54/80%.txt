Former Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic once again faces two genocide charges instead of one in his long-running trial over ethnic violence during the 1990s Balkan warsThe 1992-95 Bosnian conflict was the longest of the wars spawned by the breakup of YugoslaviaKaradzic, whose trial began in 2010, also faces nine other charges related to ethnic violence during the breakup of Yugoslavia in the 1990sThe reinstated charge accuses Karadzic of trying to permanently remove Bosnian Muslims and Croats from parts of Bosnia and Herzegovina in 1992Nearly 8,000 Muslim men and boys were killed in the Bosnian town of Srebrenica in July 1995.
