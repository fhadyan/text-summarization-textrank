 A discourse strategy is a strategy for communicating with another agent.  Agents make strategy choices via decisions about when to talk, when to let the other agent talk, what to say, and how to say it.  One choice a conversational agent must make is whether an utterance should include some relevant, but optional, information in what is communicated.
