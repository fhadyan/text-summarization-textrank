 In this paper, we argue that Higher-Order Unification (HOU) provides a linguistically adequate tool for modeling the semantics of focus.  Building up on , we develop a unification-based analysis of focus which we show favourably compares with two prominent theories of focus, Rooth's Alternative Semantics and Krifka's Structured Meanings theory.  Focus is a much debated notion.
