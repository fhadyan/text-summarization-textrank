 In order to develop a practical natural language processing (NLP) system, it is essential to deal with ill-formed sentences that cannot be parsed correctly according to the grammar rules in the system.  In this paper, an ill-formed sentence means one that cannot be parsed as a unified structure.  A syntactic parser with general grammar rules is often unable to analyze not only sentences with grammatical errors and ellipses, but also long sentences, owing to their complexity.
