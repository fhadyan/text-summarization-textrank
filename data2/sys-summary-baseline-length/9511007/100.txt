 Evaluating semantic relatedness using network representations is a problem with a long history in artificial intelligence and psychology, dating back to the spreading activation approach of Quillian and Collins and Loftus .  Semantic similarity represents a special case of semantic relatedness: for example, cars and gasoline would seem to be more closely related than, say, cars and bicycles, but the latter pair are certainly more similar.
