 Part-of-speech tagging is the process of assigning grammatical categories to individual words in a corpus.  One widely used approach makes use of a statistical technique called a Hidden Markov Model (HMM   The model is defined by two collections of parameters: the transition probabilities, which express the probability that a tag follows the preceding one (or two for a second order model and the lexical probabilities, giving the probability that a word has a given tag without regard to words on either side of it.
