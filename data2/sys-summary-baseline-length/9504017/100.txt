 It is widely acknowledged that a full account of natural language utterances cannot be given in terms of only syntactic or semantic phenomena.  For example, Hirschberg has shown that in order to understand a scalar implicature, one must analyze the conversants' beliefs and intentions.  To recognize normal state implicatures one must consider mutual beliefs and plans .
