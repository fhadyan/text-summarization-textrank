 The Spoken Language Translator (SLT) is a prototype system that translates air travel (ATIS) queries from spoken English to spoken Swedish and to French.  It is constructed, with as few modifications as possible, from existing pieces of speech and language processing software.  After the overview, we describe three ways in which the language analyser makes intelligent use of the N-best list of sentence hypotheses it receives from the recognizer.  At the highest level of generality, the design of SLT has two guiding themes.
