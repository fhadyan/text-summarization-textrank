 Unlike most linguistic theories, LFG (see Kaplan and Bresnan (1982 treats grammatical relations as first class citizens.  Accordingly, it casts its linguistic analyses in terms of a composite ontology: two independent domains a domain of constituency information (c-structure and a domain of grammatical function information (f-structure) linked together in a mutually constraining manner.
